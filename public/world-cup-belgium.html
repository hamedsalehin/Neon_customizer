Everything you need to know about the Belgium Men's national team going into the World Cup this summer

History & The Transition Era

Belgium's soccer history is highlighted by their incredible Golden Generation, which reached the semi-finals of the 2018 World Cup and spent years ranked number one in the world. Led by legends like Eden Hazard, Vincent Kompany, and Kevin De Bruyne, the Red Devils became a major force in international football. As that generation transitions, Belgium is currently integrating a wave of exciting, fast young talents, aiming to build a squad capable of maintaining their place among the elite.

Tactical Setup & Style

Belgium operates under a modern, attacking philosophy, utilizing a fluid 4-3-3 or 3-4-2-1 formation. The team's style focuses on quick ball movement, midfield dominance, and using dynamic wingers to create one-on-one situations. The coaching staff has worked to combine the veteran experience of their remaining stars with the energy and speed of their younger players, creating a highly dangerous counter-attacking team.

Key Players & Young Talents

The team is marshaled by captain Kevin De Bruyne, one of the best playmakers in soccer history, whose vision, passing range, and long-range shooting are elite. Up front, Romelu Lukaku remains a powerhouse forward, providing physical strength, hold-up play, and clinical finishing. On the wings, Jeremy Doku provides explosive pace and dribbling ability, while Johan Bakayoko adds creativity. Defensively, Wout Faes and Timothy Castagne lead a rebuilding backline, shielding experienced goalkeeper Koen Casteels.

2026 World Cup Expectations

Belgium enters the 2026 tournament with slightly lower external pressure than in previous years, which could play to their advantage. The goal is to reach the quarter-finals while continuing to develop their young squad. With De Bruyne pulling the strings and a forward line filled with explosive pace, the Red Devils remain a highly dangerous team that no opponent will want to face in the knockout rounds.

Red Devils News & Updates

Belgium completed their European qualifying campaign undefeated, showing strong attacking form. Recent tactical friendly matches have focused on defensive coordination and rapid transition play. The squad reports a clean bill of health, with De Bruyne in excellent physical condition.
